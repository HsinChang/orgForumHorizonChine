简介

Founded in 2008 and organized by the Association Amicale Franco-chinoise de ParisTech (AFCP) the Horizon China Forum has been successfully organized for more than 10 editions with the support of ParisTech and the Chinese Embassy in France. The aim of this event is to provide Chinese and French students from ParisTech and other French schools with a platform to connect with companies so that they can find an internship or a job in China as in France in large international companies.

A multitude of thematic activities

The "Horizon China" Forum aims to create a link between businesses and students. Through face-to-face discussions, conferences and online advertising, both take advantage of this opportunity to get to know each other better.

Face-to-face communication
a dynamic platform for face-to-face exchange between employers and futures employees. The meeting creates the opportunity to forge a strong bond between them, a great opportunity before a job interview.
Conferences
an opportunity to present the profession of engineer. The presentation in a ParisTech school allows the company to attract the attention of students from the best French schools, while highlighting its values, its market prospects, and its core business.
Online advertising
the event's exclusive website, LinkedIn account, public account on WeChat, brochures and other media platforms and supports reinforce the image of the company in the minds of young graduates.

More than ten years of success

With a history of more than ten years, the "Horizon China" Forum has always been held on the ParisTech campuses. MINES ParisTech, Arts et métiers ParisTech, ESPCI, Télécom Paris and others hosted this wonderful event for students and international companies. In France, the Forum has become one of the largest cross-border recruitment platforms for Chinese and French companies.

Excellent and varied candidates

Each year the Forum attracts over 300 high quality candidates from various ParisTech schools and other prestigious Grandes Ecoles and universities. Not only are Chinese students present, but French students are also invited. In 2019, around 300 students / graduates participated. About half of the candidates came from engineering schools, and a third from business schools. Two-thirds had an engineering or master’s degree, and a quarter were represented by doctoral students. More than half of the candidates were looking for a job and a quarter for an internship of more than 4 months, such as the end of study project and the internship.
